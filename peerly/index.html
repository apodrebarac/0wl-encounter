Peerly — EEG poetry from The Decameron

Part of The Sensuality of Data — PhD research by Ami Podrebarac · 0wlpoetry.com

During the COVID-19 pandemic, living in isolation with my cat Peerly, I attached EEG sensors from a PLUX biosignalsplux kit to map his three most consistent activities: eating, sleeping, and playing. Each neural spike triggered a word from Boccaccio's The Decameron — a text whose historical context of plague, storytelling, and survival mirrored the crisis unfolding around us. The quiet, cyclical rhythms of a cat's daily life became the generative engine for a body of poetry that carries traces of both cross-species neural activity and fourteenth-century narrative.
